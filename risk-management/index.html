Why It Matters

Why Most Strategies Fail

Many business failures are not caused by poor ideas. They happen because risks are identified too late, underestimated or never evaluated systematically. Effective strategy begins with understanding uncertainty before making critical decisions.

Poor Strategic Decisions

Direction chosen without a clear view of what could go wrong.

Limited Market Understanding

Assumptions about customers or competitors left untested.

Execution Risks

A sound strategy undermined by how it is carried out.

Growth Without Preparation

Scaling faster than the systems built to support it.

Categories

Types of Business Risks

Strategic Risk

Risk arising from the direction and priorities a business chooses.

Financial Risk

Exposure related to cash flow, funding and financial structure.

Operational Risk

Disruption arising from processes, people or systems.

Market Risk

Shifts in demand, pricing or market conditions.

Competitive Risk

Loss of position relative to existing or new competitors.

Technology Risk

Exposure tied to technical dependencies and infrastructure decisions.

Regulatory Risk

Exposure to changing legal or regulatory requirements.

Execution Risk

The gap between a strategic plan and how it is delivered.

Partnership Risk

Dependency on external partners, vendors or key relationships.

Investment Risk

Exposure tied to capital allocation and funding decisions.

Growth Risk

Strain created when growth outpaces business systems.

Product Risk

Uncertainty in whether a product meets real market need.

Framework

My Strategic Risk Assessment Framework

  1. 01

    Risk Identification

    Surface the risks relevant to this specific business and stage.

  2. 02

    Risk Assessment

    Evaluate each risk by likelihood and potential business impact.

  3. 03

    Risk Prioritization

    Rank risks so attention goes where it matters most.

  4. 04

    Mitigation Planning

    Define practical actions to reduce or manage each priority risk.

  5. 05

    Monitoring

    Track how identified risks evolve as the business moves forward.

  6. 06

    Review & Continuous Improvement

    Revisit the assessment as the business and market change.

Methodology

Frameworks Used

ISO 31000

An international standard for principles and guidelines on risk management.

COSO ERM

A framework connecting enterprise risk management to strategy and performance.

SWIFT Analysis

A structured what-if technique for identifying potential failure points.

PESTEL

Assesses political, economic, social, technological, environmental and legal factors.

Scenario Planning

Prepares strategy for multiple plausible futures.

Risk Heat Map

Visualises risks by likelihood and impact at a glance.

Decision Tree Analysis

Maps decisions and their possible outcomes and consequences.

Monte Carlo Simulation (Conceptual)

Explores a range of possible outcomes under uncertainty.

Risk Register

A structured, ongoing record of identified risks and their status.

Business Impact Analysis

Assesses the consequences of disruption to critical business functions.
