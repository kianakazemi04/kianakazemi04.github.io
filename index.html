What I Help Founders Achieve

Fewer opinions. Better decisions.

Most founders don't lack ideas — they lack a structured way to test them against reality before capital, time, or credibility is on the line. That's the gap I work in.

Clarity

A strategy you can defend

Not a deck built to impress a room — a direction you can explain to a co-founder, a board, or an investor in one breath, and hold to when things get uncomfortable.

Speed

Fewer cycles wasted deciding

A second, senior perspective in the room shortens the distance between "we should probably" and "we did." Decisions get made and owned, not re-litigated for months.

Coverage

The risks nobody assigned

Every growing company accumulates blind spots — market, financial, operational, reputational. Someone has to own finding them before an investor or a competitor does.

Core Expertise

Where I add the most leverage

Business Strategy

Turning ambition into a sequence of decisions — what to build, who to serve, and what to deliberately not do this year.

Business Growth

Diagnosing where growth is actually stalling — acquisition, retention, pricing, or positioning — and fixing the right one first.

Fundraising Readiness

Getting the narrative, numbers, and data room in a state that survives real investor scrutiny, not just a friendly first call.

Founder Support

A confidential second opinion for the decisions founders can't fully discuss with their own team or board.

Market Entry

Sequencing entry into a new market or vertical based on evidence — regulation, competitive density, and unit economics — not enthusiasm.

Strategic Risk Management

Mapping exposure across the business before it becomes an incident, and building the governance to keep it that way.
