Methodology

Strategic Thinking Process

Every engagement follows a structured methodology designed to reduce uncertainty and improve decision quality.

  1. 01

    Research

    Gather evidence on the market, customers and competitive landscape.

  2. 02

    Analyze

    Interpret findings to identify patterns, risks and opportunities.

  3. 03

  6. 06

    Track outcomes and refine direction as the business evolves.

Strategy

Business Strategy Frameworks

SWOT

Evaluates strengths, weaknesses, opportunities and threats.

Typical Use Assessing a business's current position before setting direction.

PESTEL

Assesses political, economic, social, technological, environmental and legal factors.

Typical Use Understanding external forces shaping a market or industry.

Porter's Five Forces

Analyses competitive pressure within an industry.

Typical Use Evaluating how attractive and defensible a market position is.

Business Model Canvas

Maps the core building blocks of a business model.

Typical Use Structuring or redesigning how a business creates and captures value.

Lean Canvas

Adapts the business model canvas for early-stage validation.

Typical Use Testing a new business idea before committing significant resources.

JTBD

Frames customer behaviour around the job being hired for.

Typical Use Understanding why customers actually choose a product or service.

Blue Ocean Strategy

Seeks uncontested market space rather than competing head-on.

Typical Use Identifying differentiated positioning in a crowded market.

Value Proposition Canvas

Aligns customer needs with product and service value.

Typical Use Sharpening messaging and product-market fit.

Balanced Scorecard

Tracks performance across financial and non-financial perspectives.

Typical Use Aligning strategic goals with day-to-day execution.

OKRs

Connects objectives to measurable, prioritised key results.

Typical Use Setting and tracking company or team priorities.

KPIs

Defines the indicators used to track meaningful performance.

Typical Use Monitoring progress against strategic goals.

RACI Matrix

Clarifies responsibility and accountability across a team or project.

Typical Use Removing ambiguity in cross-functional execution.

Scenario Planning

Prepares strategy for multiple plausible futures.

Typical Use Stress-testing strategy against different market conditions.

Risk Matrix

Maps risks by likelihood and potential business impact.

Typical Use Prioritising which risks need mitigation first.

Decision Tree Analysis

Maps decisions and their possible outcomes and consequences.

Typical Use Evaluating options with uncertain, branching outcomes.

McKinsey 7S

Aligns strategy, structure, systems and other internal factors.

Typical Use Diagnosing why strategy and execution aren't aligned.

Ansoff Matrix

Maps growth options across products and markets.

Typical Use Deciding between market penetration, expansion or diversification.

BCG Matrix

Categorises a portfolio by market growth and share.

Typical Use Prioritising investment across multiple products or business lines.

VRIO Framework

Evaluates whether a resource creates sustainable competitive advantage.

Typical Use Identifying which capabilities are genuinely defensible.

Gap Analysis

Compares current state against a desired future state.

Typical Use Identifying what stands between a business and its goals.

Marketing & Growth

Marketing & Growth Frameworks

STP

Segments a market, targets the right group and positions accordingly.

Marketing Mix (4Ps)

Structures decisions around product, price, place and promotion.

Go-To-Market Strategy

Plans how a product or service reaches its target market.

Customer Journey Mapping

Visualises the customer experience across every touchpoint.

Brand Pyramid

Structures brand identity from attributes up to core essence.

Growth Loops

Models how existing users drive acquisition of new ones.

North Star Metric

Identifies the single metric that best reflects sustainable value delivered.

AARRR

Tracks acquisition, activation, retention, referral and revenue.

Pirate Metrics

Prioritises growth experiments across the full customer funnel.

HEART Framework

Measures happiness, engagement, adoption, retention and task success.

Jobs To Be Done (Marketing Perspective)

Frames messaging around the outcome customers are trying to achieve.

Customer Segmentation

Groups customers by shared needs or behaviour to guide strategy.

Financial & Investment

Financial & Investment Thinking

Unit Economics

Assesses the profitability of a single customer or transaction.

CAC

Measures the cost of acquiring a new customer.

LTV

Estimates the total value a customer generates over time.

Burn Rate

Tracks how quickly a business spends available capital.

Runway

Estimates how long a business can operate before running out of cash.

Break-even Analysis

Identifies the point at which revenue covers costs.

Sensitivity Analysis

Tests how outcomes change when key assumptions shift.

Scenario Analysis

Compares outcomes across best-case, base-case and worst-case paths.

Financial Assumptions

Makes the reasoning behind a financial model explicit and testable.

Investment Readiness

Assesses how prepared a business is for investor scrutiny.

NPV (Conceptual)

Weighs future cash flows against their value today.

IRR (Conceptual)

Estimates the rate of return an investment is expected to generate.
